# ASEAN Quantum Summit 2025 
📆Date: 11 December 2025  
🕑Time: 2pm - 5:30pm  
📍Location: Dewan Sultan Iskandar, UTM  
<br>

## 🤔 Introduction and Objectives 
This encounter marked a difference as it emerged as a fully-fledged academic conference, unlike other events. This marked the first official summit focused on “quantum” to be organized on the Southeast Asian continent. It was organized jointly by the Universiti Teknologi Malaysia and the International Year of Quantum Science and Technology. The main aim of organizing the summit was to increase public awareness of and interest in “quantum technology.” Generally, people find these niche areas to be quite remote from their day-to-day activities, but these events bring about vital opportunities and open new avenues for the younger generation.
<br>

## 🧠 Reflection
The topics covered in this event included quantum computing, quantum communication, and the education and entrepreneurial ecosystem. It was observed that the participants were not limited to scholars from the university; individuals interested in research, the technology industry, and the general evolution of quantum mechanics also attended.

After the welcome speech by the Vice-Chancellor of UTM and the officiating speech by the Chief Minister, Malaysia’s commitment to becoming a regional leader in quantum science was noted. This focus involves strengthening research and attracting international collaboration. A major highlight was the MoU Exchange between UTM and the Center for Quantum Information and Quantum Biology. It was realized that students may have future opportunities to join global research networks, internships, or collaborative projects.

The presentation by Prof. Dr. Masahiro Kitagawa was especially impressive, covering Japan’s quantum strategy, innovation, and the challenges of Fault-Tolerant QC. Throughout these sessions, it was learned how quantum computing could solve complex natural problems, such as biological nitrogen fixation and photosynthesis carbon fixation. This was the first time hearing about such applications. Insights into Singapore’s national quantum direction were also provided by Mr. Ling Keok Tong. Through these sessions, an understanding was gained regarding the quantum future planning of both Japan and Singapore.

The exhibition booth visit was considered the most exciting part of the event, as research posters, demonstrations, and technology displays were explored. This activity provided a look at actual projects and collaborations within the region. Overall, a better understanding of how professionals view quantum technology was gained. It is hoped that more university students will become familiar with quantum technology in the future.
