## 📌 About the Talk
This industry session was conducted by PPG, a global leader in coatings, paints and specialty materials. The talk covered:

- Introduction to PPG and its global presence
- Data Analytics
- Global Infrastructure
- SAP Overview  
- Internship and employment opportunities for students

---

## 📝 Reflection
This presentation talk gave me a clearer understanding of the role of PPG. I knew that PPG is one of only a few companies that will supply paint and coating services to enhance and protect the world's leading products and brands. For example, devices and screens all around us and the cans we drink from. An introduction presentation about PPG revealed it as a large corporation that holds an immense passion for technological evolution. In the data analysis session, I saw that the data analysis team need to master in programming skills, data & file types, data visualization, ELT, DevOps practices & version control and database design. The most important skill is to be proficient in SQL and database design. I began to understand that data analysis requires not only technical skills but also logical thinking and problem-solving skills. The presentation on global infrastructure showed me that the central area for the supporting system includes cloud architecture, cloud platform engineering, LAN/WAN, servers & visualization, Internet & security and database. Besides that, the SAP presentation highlighted the important role of enterprise resource planning in managing processes, supply chains and collaboration in large organizations. These four sections made me realize that the needs for technology roles are broad and each area is connected with others.

All in all, this presentation reminded me that preparing for a future career requires more than just academic knowledge, it also requires additional skills. During the Q&A session, a student asked a very important question: 
>"If a company had to choose between two interns with one having perfect CGPA grades but just a few projects and the other with average grades but a very extensice portfolio and presence on GitHub, which candidate would the company select?"
  
The response from PPG is that both are important. Companies not just look at grades, but also consider project experience, extracurricular activities, soft & hard skills and interview performance. They emphasized that individual who able to apply their knowledge to real-world situations have a greater advantage. This statement really resonated with me because it reminded me that I not only need to be good at learning and passing exams but to apply what I learn to be practical.

---

## 🤪 Thoughts & Impressions
After this sharing session, I show increasing interest in the PPG industry. PPG has always been one of my ideal internship companies because I found that the company has a full set of technologies. In my opinion, I believe that the profession still has room for developments that suit more companies. Hence, learning more in the profession will bring me a better prospect.

Apart from technology, I realized that the company focuses on skills, attitudes, project experiences and a passion for learning, not just grades. 

Furthermore, the location of the company is very close to my home, and this is very convenient for my internship in the future and makes me even more excited to have this opportunity to join the PPG. I hope that one day I can learn more skills at PPG and contribute my abilities to the team!
