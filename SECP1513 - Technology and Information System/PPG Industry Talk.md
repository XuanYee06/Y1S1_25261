## 📌 About the Talk

This industry session was conducted by PPG, a global leader in coatings, paints and specialty materials. The talk covered:

- Introduction to PPG and its global presence
- Data Analytics
- Global Infrastructure
- SAP Overview  
- Internship and employment opportunities for students

---

## 📝 Reflection
This presentation talk gave me a clearer understanding of the role of PPG. I knew that PPG is one of only a few companies that will supply paints and coatings to enhance and protect some of the world's best-known products and brands. For example, devices and screens all around us and the cans we drink from. An introduction to PPG’s background revealed it as a large global company that highly values technological transformation. In the data analysis session, I saw that the data analysis team needed to master programming languages, data & file types, data visualization, ELT, DevOps practices & version control and database design. The most important skill is to be proficient in SQL and database design. I began to understand that data analysis requires not only technical skills but also logical thinking and problem-solving skills. The presentation on global infrastructure showed me that cloud architecture, cloud platform engineering, LAN/WAN, servers & visualization, Internet & security and database are the core of the supporting system. Besides that, the SAP presentation highlighted the important role of enterprise resource planning in managing processes, supply chains and collaboration in large organizations. These four sections made me realize that the needs for technology roles are broad and that each area is interconnected.   

All in all, this presentation reminded me that preparing for a future career requires more than just academic knowledge, it also requires additional skills. During the Q&A session, a student asked a very important question: "If a company had to choose between two interns, one with perfect CGPA grades but just a few projects and another with less impressive grades but a rich portfolio and extensive GitHub presence, who would the company choose?" PPG’s answer is that both are important. Companies don’t just look at grades, they also consider project experience, extracurricular activities, soft & hard skills and interview performance. They also emphasize that people who can apply their knowledge to real-world situations have a greater advantage. This statement really resonated with me because it reminded me that just being good at passing exams isn’t enough, I must learn how to turn my knowledge into practical skills.


---

## 🤪 Thoughts & Impressions
After this sharing session, I became more and more interested in PPG industry. PPG has always been one of my ideal internship companies because I found that it has a complete technology system, mature workflow and a clear job skill requirements. In my opinion, I think this field will continue to grow and be in line with the needs of more companies, so learning here will give me a better career development path.

Beyond technology, I also saw opportunities in the company culture. I saw that the company values abilities, attitudes, project experience, and a passion for learning, not just grades. I think this approach is fair and encourages those who are always learning and improving.

Furthermore, the company's location is very close to my home, which is very suitable for my future internship and makes me even more excited to have the opportunity to join the team. I hope that one day I can learn more practical skills at PPG and contribute my abilities to the team.
