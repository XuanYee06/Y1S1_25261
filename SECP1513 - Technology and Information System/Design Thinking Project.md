## 🧠 Reflection
The development of the digital prototype (UniWell) using Canva took six (6) weeks, in which the five (5) stages of Design Thinking were used to focus on tracking and monitor the engagement and wellness of students. A systematic procedure to collect data from seventy (70) survey responses and four (4) interviewed people resulted in a prevalence rate of stress at 37.1%. The systematic procedure in which the project progressed took four (4) scheduled meetings, which were important checkpoints in the process to make sure that the developed solution remained people-centric. By assigning tasks to four (4) team members, depending on their strength, a digital model comprising nine (9) essential functionalities of UniWell could be developed, which then proved functional to three (3) testers from the students' community. The whole experience led to an important realization that wellness analytics need to be incorporated into the customary academic tracking system.
