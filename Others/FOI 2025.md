# Festival of Ideas (FOI) 2025 

📆 **Date:** 4 – 7 November 2025  
📍 **Venue:** Putrajaya International Convention Centre (PICC)  
🎤 **Organised by:** Ministry of Higher Education (MOHE), Malaysia   

---

## 🎯 About FOI 2025  
Festival of Ideas (FOI) 2025 is an annual event that celebrates innovation, creativity, and collaboration among universities, industries and communities. It held at the Putrajaya International Convention Centre (PICC), the event featured forums, exhibitions, workshops and showcases from local and international participants. The goal of FOI is to promote innovative thinking and knowledge sharing to empower youth and students to turn their ideas into a reality that would develop the nation.  

---

## My 4-Day Journey  
### 🏛️ **Day 1 – Opening Ceremony & Exploration**  
- Attended the official opening ceremony, marking the beginning of the festival.  
- Explored various innovation booths and exhibitions including:
  - Research & Innovation Exhibition  
  - HEIs@FOI Pavilion  
  - TVET Showcase  
  - Industry Showcase  
- Learned about projects from universities and industries focusing on AI, sustainability, digital transformation and entrepreneurship.  

---

### 🍰 **Day 2 – Hands-On Workshops**  
- Joined interactive workshops where I decorate small cupcakes and learned popcorn packaging.  
- This activity taught me the importance of creativity and attention to detail.  
- It was a fun and relaxing way to experience innovation through practical learning.

## 🧠 Reflection  
Over the four days at PICC, I was exposed to innovation in a way that felt fun and interactive. It was exciting to visit each booth, interact with exhibitors, and see how different fields like AI, sustainability, culture and creative design could intertwine with each other to determine or shape the future.

One of the memorable experiences for me was taking part in hands-on workshops where I decorated mini cupcakes and learned how to package popcorn. Besides that, the speeches and forums were especially impactful. Listening to the speechs by experts on digital transformation, aerospace innovation and future of Malaysia have opened my eyes to know how fast the world is changing. Even getting to see our Prime Minister, Dato’ Seri Anwar Ibrahim, and hearing him give a speech about youth innovation was a very special moment.

Another part that made FOI special was the journey itself. We took bus from UTM to Putrajaya every morning. I think it was really fun doing something different from the usual routine of university life. Compared to our usual routine of attending classes and returning to dorm, it was more of an adventure.

All in all, FOI 2025 left a strong impression on me. It reminded me that learning is not only about lectures and assignments, it is also about exploring, interacting with people and experiencing new environments. It gave me the opportunity to engage with ideas and with my classmates to understand how innovations are used to make a difference and how my ideas can be transformed into innovations using technology as a Data Engineer.
